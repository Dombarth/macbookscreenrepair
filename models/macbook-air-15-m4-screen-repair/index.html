MacBook Air 15" M4 Screen Repair

At MacBook Screen Repair we handle the MacBook Air 15 inch M4 released in 2025. This is model identifier A3201. The team provide LCD only replacement and full display assembly replacement depending on the condition of your MacBook Air when you bring it in.

The 15 inch MacBook Air with M4 chip has a 15.3 inch Liquid Retina display with 2880 x 1864 resolution. Same panel technology as the M3 generation with P3 wide colour gamut and True Tone support. When the screen gets damaged we can replace either just the LCD panel or the complete display assembly.

About the 15 inch Display

The MacBook Air 15" M4 has the larger display that Apple introduced with the M2 generation. 15.3 inches of screen real estate with slim bezels and the notch at the top for the camera. The extra screen size makes it popular with people who want more space without going to a MacBook Pro.

Inside the display assembly theres the LCD panel, backlight, camera module and display cables. The larger panel means more surface area that can get damaged but the repair process is similar to the 13 inch model. We swap the panel or the whole assembly depending on whats needed.

LCD Only Replacement

LCD only replacement for the MacBook Air 15" M4 means we swap just the internal LCD panel while keeping your original display housing. Metal frame stays in place. Camera module stays. Backlight assembly and cabling all stays. We just remove the damaged panel and fit a new one in its place.

This repair is available when the display housing meets the eligibility requirements. No dents, no bends, no warping in the frame. No liquid exposure around the display. Bezel intact around all edges and corners. We assess all of this when you drop off.

LCD only is what we prefer to do when the housing allows it. More targeted repair. Keeps your original housing and camera. True Tone usually stays working because the original electronics remain in place.

Full Display Replacement

Full display replacement is necessary when the housing has damage. Dented frame, bent corners, liquid has gotten into the display area, cracked bezels around the edges. In these cases LCD only wont work properly and full replacement is required.

With full display replacement we swap the entire lid assembly. New housing, new LCD panel, new camera module, new backlight, new cables. The whole lot. Its a bigger repair than LCD only but sometimes thats what the MacBook needs.

Full display replacement can affect True Tone functionality. Apple pairs this feature to the original display through firmware. The replacement assembly doesnt have this pairing and independent repairers cant restore it. Everything else works fine just True Tone may not be available.

Common Issues

We see similar screen issues on the 15 inch MacBook Air as we do on the 13 inch. Cracked screens from drops and impacts. The larger display means more surface area to potentially crack if it takes a hit. Dead displays, flickering, lines across the screen, backlight problems.

The larger size also means its a bit more awkward to carry around which can lead to more accidental drops. Weve seen alot of these come in with cracked corners from being dropped while carrying between rooms or getting knocked off tables.

Identifying Your Model

The MacBook Air 15" M4 is model A3201. Check your model by clicking the Apple menu and selecting About This Mac. It shows the model name and year. The model identifier is also on the bottom of the machine near the regulatory markings.

If your not sure whether you have the 15 inch or 13 inch bring it in and we can check. The size difference is obvious when you see them side by side but the model numbers confirm it.

MacBook Air 15" M4 Screen Repair Questions

Can you repair a cracked MacBook Air 15" M4 screen?
Yes we repair cracked screens on the MacBook Air 15" M4. Either LCD only replacement if the housing is undamaged or full display replacement if there is frame damage.
Is the 15 inch repair more expensive than 13 inch?
The larger panel does cost more than the 13 inch. We give you exact pricing at assessment based on your specific MacBook and what repair it needs.
Will True Tone still work after repair?
LCD only replacement usually preserves True Tone. Full display replacement can affect True Tone because Apple ties this to the original display hardware.
How long does the repair take?
Most LCD only repairs are same day if parts are in stock. Full display replacement may take longer. We give timeframes at assessment.
