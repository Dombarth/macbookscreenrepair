MacBook Pro 13" 2017 Touch Bar Display Overview

The 2017 MacBook Pro 13" with Touch Bar continued the design Apple introduced late 2016. Model identifier A1706 applies to both 2016 and 2017 Touch Bar equipped 13" models, with the 2017 version bringing 7th generation Kaby Lake processors. Four Thunderbolt 3 ports and the Touch Bar differentiated this from the function key model. These machines represented Apple's vision for pro notebooks though the design drew some criticism for port selection and keyboard reliability.

Mac Screen Repair Sydney specialise in the aspects of Retina display repair for A1706 MacBook Pro models. The 13" Retina display runs at 2560 x 1600 with P3 colour gamut support. We've repaired alot of these Touch Bar models since they launched. Understanding the internal architecture and common failure points helps us deliver reliable repairs.

Screen Repair Methods

Two repair approaches address screen damage on 13" 2017 Touch Bar models. LCD only replacement involves extracting the damaged internal panel while keeping your original aluminium housing intact. This requires housing free from structural damage. Full display assembly replacement swaps the complete top section when housing dents or bends make LCD only unsuitable.

The team at Mac Screen Repair Sydney evaluate your device before suggesting either option. We dont push assembly replacements when LCD only delivers equivalent results. Your housing condition and damage pattern determine our recommendation. Honest evaluation means appropriate repair.

Display Technical Specifications

The A1706 features a 13.3-inch LED-backlit IPS Retina display at 2560 x 1600 native resolution. This delivers 227 pixels per inch for sharp text and detailed images. The panel supports P3 wide colour gamut for accurate colour reproduction in creative applications. Maximum brightness reaches 500 nits which handles typical lighting well.

The 2017 13" Touch Bar model does not include True Tone technology. This feature wasnt added until the 2018 refresh. For repair purposes, this simplifies considerations since theres no True Tone calibration data to account for. Both repair methods deliver identical display functionality after repair.

No True Tone Considerations

Since the 2017 13" Touch Bar lacks True Tone, repair decisions dont involve preserving that functionality. Both LCD only and full assembly replacement restore your display to full operation without any features lost. This straightforward outcome makes repair planning simpler than on later True Tone equipped models.

The display still provides excellent P3 colour gamut coverage and accurate reproduction for professional work. Replacement panels match original specifications for colour accuracy and brightness levels.

Known Flexgate Issues

The 2016-2017 13" Touch Bar models became known for display cable failures commonly called flexgate. Apple's design routed the display cable through the hinge area where repeated opening and closing creates wear over time. Symptoms include a bright band at the screen bottom, flickering, or complete display failure at certain angles. The problem typically worsens progressively.

Apple acknowledged this issue and extended warranty coverage for affected units. Many machines now fall outside that program. The team have repaired numerous flexgate cases on A1706 models. Full display assembly replacement addresses cable issues definitively since it includes new cables. We diagnose whether symptoms indicate cable wear or panel damage.

Common Display Damage

Beyond flexgate, physical damage causes most repairs. Impact damage from drops creates cracked glass and LCD fractures. Pressure damage from bags causes panel failures without external cracks. The thin display assembly provides less protection than older thicker designs. Objects pressing on closed MacBooks create alot of the pressure damage we repair.

We accurately diagnose damage type since this affects repair approach. Cracked screens show visible damage patterns while cable issues manifest as backlight or intermittent failures without physical damage.

Butterfly Keyboard Context

The A1706 uses the butterfly keyboard mechanism that gained notoriety for reliability issues. Screen repairs dont touch keyboard components at all. If you have separate keyboard issues, thats a different repair matter. Apple extended service programs may cover certain keyboard failures. Our screen repairs focus entirely on display components.

Despite keyboard concerns, the machines perform well otherwise. Screen repair makes sense when the rest of the system works properly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is A1706 the same as the 2016 model?

Yes, both 2016 and 2017 Touch Bar 13" models share the A1706 identifier and use identical display assemblies. Parts are interchangeable between model years.

What is flexgate and is this model affected?

Flexgate refers to display cable failures from wear at the hinge. The 2016-2017 Touch Bar models are the most commonly affected. Symptoms include stage lighting, flickering, or display blackouts.

Does this model have True Tone?

No, the 2017 13" Touch Bar does not include True Tone. This feature wasnt added to the 13" until the 2018 model. Repair outcomes are identical regardless of method.

How do I know if my issue is flexgate or a cracked screen?

Cracked screens show visible physical damage. Flexgate symptoms include backlight failure at the bottom, flickering without physical damage, or issues that change with lid angle.
