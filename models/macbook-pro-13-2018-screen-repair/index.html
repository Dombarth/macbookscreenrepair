MacBook Pro 13" 2018 (A1989) Display Overview

The 2018 MacBook Pro 13" brought substantial upgrades to the Touch Bar lineup. Model identifier A1989 covers both 2018 and 2019 four port models. The 2018 release introduced True Tone display technology to the 13" MacBook Pro, matching the feature set of that years 15" model. Quad-core 8th generation Intel processors replaced the previous dual-core chips, delivering meaningful performance improvements. The T2 security chip also debuted in this generation.

Screen Repair Options

Two repair methods suit the 13" 2018 depending on your damage. LCD only replacement removes the damaged internal panel while keeping your original aluminium housing and camera assembly. This requires housing free from dents or bends. Full display assembly replacement swaps the entire top section when housing damage prevents LCD only repair.

The team at Mac Screen Repair Sydney assess devices before recommending either path. We dont default to expensive assembly replacements when LCD only achieves the same result. Housing condition and damage extent drive our recommendation. You get appropriate repair without unnecessary cost.

True Tone Introduction

The 2018 13" MacBook Pro was the first 13" model with True Tone technology. This feature adjusts display white balance based on ambient lighting conditions, aiming to reduce eye strain during extended use. True Tone data lives in the original display housing assembly. This matters for repair decisions since different methods affect True Tone differently.

LCD only replacement preserves your original housing where True Tone calibration data resides. The feature continues working after this repair type. Full assembly replacement introduces new hardware without your original True Tone data. The feature wont function automatically after assembly replacement.

Display Technical Specifications

The A1989 features a 13.3-inch LED-backlit IPS Retina display at 2560 x 1600 native resolution. This provides 227 pixels per inch for sharp text and detailed imagery. The panel supports P3 wide colour gamut for accurate colour reproduction in creative work. Maximum brightness reaches 500 nits which handles typical lighting environments adequately.

Common Display Issues

Physical damage accounts for most screen repairs we perform on A1989 2018 models. Impact damage from drops creates cracked glass and LCD fractures. Pressure damage from bags causes panel failures without visible external cracks. The team have seen screens with impact points, spreading crack patterns, and localised LCD failure.

The 2018-2019 13" Touch Bar models can develop display cable wear from repeated opening and closing. Symptoms include flickering, display blackouts at certain angles, or stage lighting at the bottom edge. The cable design differs from 2016-2017 models but issues can still develop over time. We diagnose whether problems stem from cable versus panel damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the first 13" with True Tone?

Yes, the 2018 A1989 was the first 13" MacBook Pro to include True Tone display technology. Previous 13" models lacked this automatic white balance feature.

Is the 2018 display same as 2019?

Yes, both 2018 and 2019 four port models share the A1989 identifier and use identical display assemblies. Parts are interchangeable between these years.

Will True Tone work after repair?

With LCD only repair, True Tone continues functioning since calibration data stays in the original housing. Full assembly replacement means True Tone becomes inactive.

Is quad-core a big improvement?

Yes, the 2018 quad-core models deliver significantly better performance than previous dual-core 13" MacBook Pro generations. This makes them worth repairing for continued use.
