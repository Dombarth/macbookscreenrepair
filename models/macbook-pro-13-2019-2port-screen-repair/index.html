MacBook Pro 13" 2019 (A2159) Display Overview

The 2019 MacBook Pro 13" with two Thunderbolt 3 ports marked the first time Apple brought Touch Bar to the entry level 13" configuration. Model identifier A2159 designates this specific variant which replaced the non-Touch Bar models from previous years. With 8th generation Intel processors and integrated graphics, this model targeted users wanting Touch Bar functionality at a more accessible price point. Screen damage doesnt mean end of life for these capable machines.

Mac Screen Repair Sydney specialise in the aspects of Retina display repair for A2159 MacBook Pro models. The 13" display delivers 2560 x 1600 resolution with True Tone support. We've worked on alot of these entry level 2019 Touch Bar units and understand the repair process thoroughly. Quality parts ensure your display performs correctly after repair.

Available Repair Methods

Two repair paths address screen damage on the 13" 2019 two port model. LCD only replacement extracts the damaged internal panel while keeping your original aluminium housing and camera assembly intact. This requires housing without structural damage. Full display assembly replacement swaps the complete top section when housing dents or bends prevent LCD only repair.

The team at Mac Screen Repair Sydney evaluate your device before suggesting either option. We dont recommend full assembly replacements when LCD only achieves equivalent results. Your housing condition and damage type determine our recommendation. You get honest assessment focused on appropriate repair.

Display Technical Specifications

The A2159 features a 13.3-inch LED-backlit IPS Retina display running at 2560 x 1600 native resolution. This delivers 227 pixels per inch for crisp text and detailed images. The panel supports P3 wide colour gamut for accurate colour reproduction and includes True Tone technology. Maximum brightness reaches 500 nits which handles typical lighting conditions well.

This was the first two port model to include True Tone, which arrived with the Touch Bar on the entry level configuration. Display quality matches the higher spec four port models identically. Replacement panels maintain the same specifications for colour accuracy and brightness.

True Tone Status After Repair

True Tone functionality depends on repair method. LCD only replacement preserves your original housing assembly where True Tone calibration data lives. The feature continues working normally after this type of repair. Full assembly replacement means new hardware without your original True Tone data, so the feature wont function automatically.

For users who value True Tone, this distinction matters when choosing repair options. Most everyday users find True Tone absence has minimal practical impact. We discuss these details during assessment so theres no confusion about what to expect.

Common Display Problems

Physical damage causes most repairs we see on A2159 models. Impacts from drops create cracked glass and LCD fractures. Pressure damage from bags causes panel failures without visible external cracks. The team have repaired screens with single impact points, spreading crack patterns, and broad LCD failure.

The A2159 benefits from improved display cable design compared to earlier Touch Bar generations. Apple addressed flexgate concerns with revised cable routing. Cable failures are less common on this model though not impossible. We diagnose whether issues stem from cable wear versus panel damage to ensure correct repair approach.

First Touch Bar Entry Model

The A2159 marked the first time Touch Bar appeared on the entry level 13" MacBook Pro. Previous two port models used function keys. This brought Touch Bar features to a broader audience while keeping price lower than four port configurations. Screen repairs dont affect Touch Bar functionality at all since its a separate component in the bottom assembly.

The introduction of Touch Bar to entry level also brought T2 chip and True Tone to this price segment. These features continue working through our screen repairs.

T2 Security Chip

The A2159 includes Apple's T2 security chip managing encryption, secure boot, and TouchID. Screen repairs dont interact with T2 functionality since the chip resides on the logic board separate from display components. Your data remains encrypted and secure. TouchID continues functioning normally. Security settings stay intact through the repair process.

We follow proper procedures during disassembly to avoid triggering any security concerns. Your MacBook returns ready for immediate use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is A2159 the same as the four port model?

No, A2159 is the two port model with different specifications and display assembly. A1989 is the four port 2019 model. Parts are not interchangeable between these configurations.

Did this model fix flexgate issues?

Apple improved the display cable design in the A2159 compared to earlier Touch Bar models. Flexgate failures are significantly less common though not completely eliminated.

Is this the first two port model with Touch Bar?

Yes, the A2159 was the first entry level two port 13" MacBook Pro to include Touch Bar. Previous two port models used traditional function keys.

Will True Tone still work after repair?

With LCD only repair, True Tone continues functioning normally. Full assembly replacement means the feature becomes inactive since calibration data doesnt transfer.
